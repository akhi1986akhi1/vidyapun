The duties in a career as an Illustrator are as follows –

  • Illustrators meet with clients and project managers to discuss the specifications of a project. Illustrators first consult with a customer to go through the design and project specifications.
  • If they are employed by an organization, they might meet with a project manager or team lead to get instructions on their task.
  • The illustrator and client establish a deadline after considering the project’s specifics. With a timeline in hand, the illustrator can use it to finish the project’s various components.
  • Also, information about salary, due dates, and project agreements are negotiated by illustrators.
  • To help them produce the desired aesthetic results, they employ several tools. Typically, illustrators create sample illustrations to present to clients in order to gauge their approval of the work and identify any adjustments that may be required.
  • Depending on the client’s response, illustrators can make adjustments to the project at this stage and change the specifications for the artwork.
  • They conduct a study to better comprehend the specifics of a project. They can also conduct studies on the ideas and subjects related to the project, such as technical words or medical notions.
  • They might get more comments from the client about the item that they can use as a guide for future work.

Job roles for an Illustrator

  • Product Illustrator:

    Product illustrators view their illustrations as a means of developing a completely distinct identity for a product and as a crucial component of the narrative that must be told for the product to succeed.

  • Fashion Illustrator:

    Almost any illustration with an emphasis on clothing, accessories, or associated media falls under the umbrella of fashion illustration. The choice of artistic medium is typically decided by the designer, and fashion illustrators may utilize realistic depictions or more abstract sketches.
